Yerba Mansa(Anemopis Californica)

Organ: Mucous Membrane

Pharmacological properties:
  1. Stimulant
  2. Asrtingent
  3. Carmenative
  4. Antiemetic
  5. Antiseptic
  6. Diuretic
  7. Anti-inflammatory
  8. Antifungal
  9. Antimicrobial
  10. Anti-mutagenic
  11. Antiproliferative

Yerba Mansa is a powerful herb of many medicinal uses. Yerba Mansa has anti-inflammatory and mucus-expelling affects in the sinus and lungs. It has a gentle warming, astringing, and stimulating action in the digestive system. With tonifying effects in the mucous membranes, yerba mansa can protect against and heal infections similarly to Hydrastis canadensis (Goldenseal). Roots and rhizomes are also useful to be ground into powder and applied as a paste or mixed with clay to disinfect and heal wounds, or gargled as tea or chewed for antiseptic and analgesic effects in oral or throat inflammations and infections. Yerba Mansa acts on the gastrointestinal, respiratory, and genitourinary mucous membranes of the body. Yerba Mansa's astringent action improves tissue integrity and checks secretions, while its stimulating activity helps remove accumulated wastes ushering in oxygen and nutrients to regenerate poorly healing membranes. In the gastrointestinal tract, it will heal mucous membrane congestion and deterioration of tissues.
