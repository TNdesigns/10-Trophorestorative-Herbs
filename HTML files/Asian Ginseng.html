Asian Ginseng(Panax Ginseng)

Organs: Nervous System, Endocrine system

Pharmacological Properties:
  1. Neuroprotective
  2. Anti-depressant
  3. Anti-inflammatory
  4. Anti-oxidant
  5. Anti-tumor
  6. Anti-diabetic
  7. Anti-psychotic
  8. Analgesic
  9. Anti-carcinogenic
  10. Antihyperglycemic

The active ingredients in ginseng are ginsenosides of which there are more than 30 different molecular structures. Ginsenoside Rb1 can reduce arterial vasospasm and brain edema and improve neurobehavioral function. Ginsenoside Rb2 possesses neuroprotective effects by suppressing glutamate-induced neurotoxicity. Ginseng may protect neurons from neurotoxicity and other damage. Ginseng total saponins (GTS) can protect brain cells, induce endogenous Neural stem cells to proliferate, and improve neurological function. Ginsenosides reduce platelet aggregation, suppress thrombin formation, and relax smooth muscle cells. Ginseng has been shown to increase blood flow in the brain, protect nerve cells from damage, and enhance nerve development, thereby improving memory and learning. The specific ginsenosides Rb1 and Rg1 are thought to increase nerve growth and protect nerve cells from damage. Ginseng regulates the hypothalamic–pituitary–adrenal (HPA) axis by ameliorating tissue injury and immune cell death, while modulating immune cells in order to limit inflammatory responses.
